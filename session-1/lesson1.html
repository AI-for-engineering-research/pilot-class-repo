Fundamentals · memory

The context window is its working memory

01

Only sees what's in front of it

Everything it can reason over must fit in the window. Nothing else exists for it.

02

Knowledge cutoff

It doesn't inherently know recent events or your private data. That's a gap, not a search.

03

It will confidently invent

Ungrounded, it hallucinates. The fix: hand it tools and retrieval so it works from real, checkable facts.

Fundamentals · the leap

From a model to an agent

A model

Takes text in, gives text out. One shot. No hands, no memory of the world.

An agent

Wraps the model in a loop: it acts (calls tools), observes results, and iterates toward a goal.

The difference is the loop and the tools — not the model.

Fundamentals · where your work lives

The harness is the scaffolding around the model

Instructions

System prompt and the rules of engagement.

Tools

What it's allowed to call — and how.

Control flow

The loop itself: when to act, retry, stop.

Memory / context

What it remembers and what you feed it.

Environment

Files, terminal, web, your codebase.

Guardrails

Limits that keep it safe and on-task.

A good harness with an okay model often beats a great model with no harness. Your engineering quality lives here.

Fundamentals · your role

You're the architect and the verifier

The agent supplies volume and speed. You supply judgment, framing, and verification. You cannot trust what you cannot check.

That single sentence is why this course invests so heavily in tests, evaluation suites, and reproducibility.
